Augmented reality

Augmented reality is an interactive experience of a real-world environment. The objects that reside in the real world are enhanced by computer-generated perceptual information, sometimes across multiple sensory modalities.

AR is a system that fulfills three features - a combination of real and virtual worlds, real-time interaction, and accurate 3D registration of virtual and real objects. This technology is in a way “reality bending”.

Camera tracking

Real-time camera tracking enables live virtual broadcast graphics in indoor or outside broadcast, including installation on a handheld, Steadicam and cable-based systems.

Tracking is the positional measurement of bodies that move in a defined space. There are many tracking systems, based on different measurement principles - mechanical trackers, magnetic trackers, optical trackers (VIS or IR), acoustic trackers and systems based on inertial or gyro sensors. Also widely used are hybrid systems, combining different techniques.

Video tracking

Video tracking is the process of locating a moving object/s using a camera. When tracking flat objects, the motion model is a 2D transformation. When the target is a non-flat 3D object, the motion model defines its aspect depending on its 3D position and orientation.
